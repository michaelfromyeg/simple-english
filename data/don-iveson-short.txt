<p><a class="key">Donald L. Iveson</a> (born May 30, 1979) is a <a class="key">Canadian politician</a> who was mayor of Edmonton from 2013 to 2021. He first won the mayoral election in 2013 with 62% of the vote, and was re-elected in 2017 with 73.6% of the vote. Before becoming mayor, Iveson was on Edmonton City Council from 2007 to 2013.</p>

<p><a class="key">Iveson</a> was born in St. Albert, Alberta in 1979. He grew up in Parkallen, Edmonton, with his parents Margaret and Bob Iveson. As a kid, he loved reading, scouting, and debate. He studied political science at the University of Alberta, where he also worked at the student newspaper. After graduating, he worked at the University of Alberta Students' Union before entering politics.</p>

<p>In 2007, <a class="key">Iveson</a> entered municipal politics and won a seat on the Edmonton City Council. He focused on public transportation, housing, and environmental issues. He later ran for mayor and won the election in 2013. During his time as mayor, he supported LGBTQ rights and environmental causes.</p>

<p><a class="key">Iveson</a> is divorced and has a son and a daughter with his ex-wife Sarah Chan. He enjoys sailing, Star Trek, and the band Arcade Fire. In 2011, he appeared in a video promoting the Edmonton Transit System.</p>
