<p>Russian is an East <a class="key">Slavic language</a> mainly spoken in Russia. It is part of the Indo-European family and one of the four living East Slavic languages. It was the official language of the former <a class="key">Soviet Union</a> and remains official in Russia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan. It is used as a <a class="key">lingua franca</a> in countries like Ukraine, Moldova, the Caucasus, and Central Asia.</p>

<p>Russian has over 258 million speakers worldwide. It is the most spoken native language in Europe and the most common Slavic language. Russian is one of the six official languages of the United Nations and is also used on the International Space Station. It is the fourth most used language on the Internet.</p>

<p>Russian is written using the Cyrillic alphabet, which distinguishes between "soft" and "hard" consonant sounds. Stress in Russian words can be unpredictable and is sometimes indicated with an acute accent.</p>

<p>Russian is a descendant of Old East Slavic, used in the Kievan Rus' from the late 9th to the mid-13th centuries. Its closest language relatives are Ukrainian, Belarusian, and Rusyn. Russian has been influenced by many languages over centuries, including Greek, Latin, and French.</p>

<p>Important features of Russian grammar include three tenses (past, present, future) and six cases for nouns. Russian verbs of motion have unique forms to indicate types of movement.</p>
