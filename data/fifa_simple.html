Internationally, soccer is governed by FIFA. Under FIFA, there are six continental groups: AFC, CAF, CONCACAF, CONMEBOL, OFC, and UEFA. CONMEBOL is the oldest one, started in 1916. National soccer associations like The FA or JFA manage the game in their countries. They take care of both professional games and amateur games. They also organize competitions based on the Laws of the Game. The most important international competitions are the FIFA World Cup and the FIFA Women's World Cup. The men's World Cup is the most-watched sporting event in the world, with more viewers than the Olympic Games.[6] In European club soccer, the most important competitions are the UEFA Champions League and the UEFA Women's Champions League. These tournaments have many viewers around the world. Since 2009, the final match of the men's Champions League has been the most-watched yearly sporting event.[7]
