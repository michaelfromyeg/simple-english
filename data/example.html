The Stanley Cup Finals is the championship series for the National Hockey League (NHL). The winning team gets the Stanley Cup, the oldest trophy in North American professional sports.

Here are some key points:

  • The Stanley Cup was donated in 1892 by Lord Stanley of Preston as a challenge trophy for top amateur teams in Canada.
  • Professional teams could challenge for it from 1906.
  • Since 1915, it is awarded to the best team each season.
  • Since 1926, it is the NHL's championship trophy.

The championship round is a best-of-seven series between the Eastern and Western Conference champions.

Key Historical Events

  • 1914: First "World Series" of ice hockey held.
  • 1926: The Cup officially becomes the NHL championship trophy.
  • 1947: NHL gains control over who competes for the Stanley Cup.

Broadcasting

  • First TV broadcast in Canada: 1953 on CBC.
  • First U.S TV broadcast: 1962 by WGN (Chicago).
  • NHL Finals currently broadcast by ABC and cable channel TNT in the U.S.

Format

  • Championship round: Best-of-seven series.
  • Current format: Eastern vs. Western Conference champions.

Recent Champions

  • 2023: Vegas Golden Knights
  • 2022: Colorado Avalanche
  • 2021: Tampa Bay Lightning
  • 2020: Tampa Bay Lightning
  • 2019: St. Louis Blues

The Montreal Canadiens have the most titles with 24 wins.
